Plastic Pollution
The marine life on mother earth is dying at an increasing rate, because of plastic pollution. Large rivers around the world contribute to 90% of the plastic pollution that ends up in our oceans. At the current rate there will be more plastic than fish in our oceans by 2050. According to Alex Gray in World Economic Forum



Information gathered from: weforum.org

Sustainable Development Goal 14
The UN has several goals aimed at making earth a better place to inhabit for everyone. Their 14th SDG is to conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources. We want to help them reach their 1st target. Which is:
By 2025, prevent and significantly reduce marine pollution of all kinds, in particular from land-based activities, including marine debris and nutrient pollution.

Which you can read more about here: UN SDG 14

Solving the problem

Our solution is based around modifying bacteria, so that they connect different types of plastic molecules to each other. This makes the vast amounts of plastic much easier to spot and fetch out of the rivers, before it reaches an ocean. So it can be disposed of in a more environmentally friendly way...
